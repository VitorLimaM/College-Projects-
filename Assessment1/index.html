The five-time world cup champion

the fifth time Brazil won the world cup on June 30, 2002

On June 30, 2002, in Japan, Brazil won its fifth world championship.

The Brazilian team nicknamed Canarinho won the final against Germany 2-0, the team was composed of coach Felipe Scolari and the 11 starters Marcos (goalkeeper), Cafu (captain), Lúcio, Roque Júnior, Edmílson, Roberto Carlos, Ricardinho (in fact, the one who played was Kléberson - Ricardinho was on the bench), Gilberto Silva, Ronaldo, Rivaldo, Ronaldinho Gaúcho, with Ronaldo or known as R9 or Ronaldo Fenomeno as the author of the 2 goals.

The Four-time world cup champion

the fourth time Brazil won the world cup on July 17, 1994

On July 17, 1994, in USA, Brazil won its fourth world championship.

The Brazilian team, nicknamed Canarinho, won the 1994 World Cup final against Italy after a 0x0 draw, defeating them 3x2 on penalties. The team was led by coach Carlos Alberto Parreira and included the 11 starters: Taffarel (goalkeeper), Jorginho, Aldair, Márcio Santos, Branco, Dunga (captain), Mauro Silva, Mazinho, Zinho, Bebeto, and Romário. Goalkeeper Taffarel made key saves during the penalty shootout, and Italys Roberto Baggio missed the decisive penalty that secured Brazils fourth World Cup title.

The Third-time world cup champion

the third time Brazil won the world cup on June 21, 1970

On June 21, 1970, in Mexico, Brazil won its thrid world championship.

The Brazilian team, nicknamed Canarinho, won the 1970 World Cup final against Italy with a 4x1 victory. The team was led by coach Mário Zagallo and included the 11 starters: Félix (goalkeeper), Carlos Alberto (captain), Brito, Piazza, Everaldo, Clodoaldo, Gérson, Tostão, Rivelino, Pelé, and Jairzinho. The goals were scored by Pelé, Gérson, Jairzinho, and Carlos Alberto, showcasing a dominant performance that secured Brazils third World Cup title, allowing them to keep the Jules Rimet Trophy permanently.
